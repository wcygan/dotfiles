# Destination Discovery Frameworks

## The Preference Extraction Matrix

Most travelers say "I want to go somewhere nice." That's not a starting point. Use this matrix to surface what they actually value.

### Core Dimensions (rank 1-5)

| Dimension | Low (1) | High (5) |
|---|---|---|
| **Novelty** | Familiar comfort, repeat visits | Completely new culture, language, environment |
| **Activity Level** | Beach + book, slow mornings | Packed days, hiking, diving, constant movement |
| **Social Density** | Solitude, quiet, few interactions | Markets, nightlife, group tours, meeting locals |
| **Structure** | No plan, pure wandering | Detailed itinerary, guided experiences |
| **Comfort** | Hostels, street food, roughing it | Nice hotels, fine dining, curated luxury |
| **Cultural Distance** | Familiar language/customs | Very different norms, potential disorientation |

### Destination Personality Quiz

Ask these questions to map preferences to destination types:

1. **Morning question**: "Your ideal morning abroad -- are you at a cafe watching the city wake up, on a trail at dawn, sleeping in at a resort, or exploring a market?"
2. **Challenge question**: "Are you energized or drained by things feeling unfamiliar?"
3. **Memory question**: "Think of your best travel memory. What made it great -- the place, the people, the food, the activity, or the surprise?"
4. **Pace question**: "Do you want to see a lot or know a place deeply?"
5. **Return question**: "Should you come back with stories or with rest?"

## Destination Type Taxonomy

### By Environment
- **Coastal/Island**: Relaxation-forward, water activities, seafood culture, slower pace
- **Mountain/Alpine**: Adventure potential, scenic grandeur, seasonal extremes, physical challenge
- **Desert**: Stark beauty, spiritual quality, extreme climate, stargazing, solitude
- **Tropical Forest**: Biodiversity, humidity, adventure, wildlife, remoteness
- **Urban/Megacity**: Density of culture, food, nightlife, museums, architecture, sensory overload
- **Small City/Town**: Walkability, local character, depth over breadth, personal encounters
- **Rural/Agricultural**: Slow pace, local food systems, landscape, authenticity, quiet
- **Arctic/Subarctic**: Northern lights, midnight sun, extreme seasons, unique wildlife

### By Experience Gravity
- **Heritage/Historical**: Ancient sites, museums, guided context, deep learning
- **Culinary Capitals**: Food scene as primary draw, markets, wine/beer regions
- **Adventure Hubs**: Proximity to multiple outdoor activities, guide infrastructure
- **Wellness Destinations**: Spas, retreats, thermal waters, meditation centers
- **Festival/Event Centers**: Plan around a specific event (see seasonal-timing.md)
- **Creative Cities**: Art, music, design, street culture, maker communities

## The "Second City" Principle

Capital cities and top tourist destinations are popular for good reason. But the second-largest city or the secondary destination often delivers:
- 80% of the cultural richness at 50% of the cost and crowds
- More authentic local life (fewer tourist-oriented businesses)
- Surprise factor -- you discover instead of confirm

Framework: For any country, research the 2nd-3rd largest city. What do locals go there for?

## Constraint-Based Filtering

When the user has hard constraints, filter fast:

| Constraint | Filters Out | Opens Up |
|---|---|---|
| Budget < $100/day | Scandinavia, Switzerland, Japan (cities) | SEA, Central America, Balkans, India |
| < 5 days | Anything requiring acclimatization, multi-stop | Weekend-distance destinations, single-city deep dives |
| Family with young kids | Extreme adventure, nightlife-focused, remote | Beach resorts, cultural cities with kid infrastructure, nature parks |
| Accessibility needs | Cobblestone old towns, wilderness, developing infrastructure | Modern cities, cruise-based exploration, resort complexes |
| Solo female traveler | Nowhere is off-limits, but some places require more preparation | Well-worn solo traveler routes, cities with strong hostel culture |
| Visa-free urgency | Countries requiring advance visa applications | Visa-on-arrival or visa-free destinations for their passport |

## Decision Anti-Patterns

- **The Instagram Trap**: Choosing a destination because it photographs well, not because it matches your interests
- **The "Everyone Goes There" Default**: Following trends instead of preferences (Bali syndrome)
- **The Distance Fallacy**: Assuming far = exotic and near = boring. Some of the best trips are 2 hours away
- **The Checklist Mentality**: "I haven't been to Africa yet" -- continents aren't destinations, countries barely are, and regions are where the real choosing happens
- **Analysis Paralysis**: Comparing 15 destinations against 20 criteria forever. After initial filtering, trust your gut on the final 2-3
