# Packing Wisdom

Pack for the trip you're taking, not every trip you might take. The best-packed bag is the lightest one that covers your actual needs.

## The Core Principle

**Lay out everything you think you need. Remove half. You'll still have too much.**

Overpacking is the most common travel mistake and the most fixable. Every unnecessary item:
- Weighs you down physically
- Costs you in checked baggage fees
- Slows every arrival and departure
- Limits spontaneity (you can't take that side trip if you're hauling 50 pounds)

## The Capsule Travel Wardrobe

### The Formula (adjustable by trip length)

| Duration | Tops | Bottoms | Layers | Shoes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 3-5 days | 3-4 | 2 | 1-2 | 2 |
| 7-10 days | 4-5 | 2-3 | 2 | 2 |
| 14+ days | 5-6 | 3 | 2-3 | 2-3 |

**Why it doesn't scale linearly**: Because you do laundry. A 3-week trip doesn't need 3x the clothes of a 1-week trip.

### The Color Strategy
Pick a base color (black, navy, khaki) and a secondary color (anything that complements it). Every piece should work with every other piece. You should be able to:
- Mix any top with any bottom
- Layer anything over anything
- Look put-together without thinking about it

### Fabric Principles
- **Merino wool**: Odor-resistant, temperature-regulating, dries fast. Worth the investment for base layers
- **Quick-dry synthetics**: For active travel. Wash in sink, dry overnight
- **Cotton**: Comfortable but slow to dry and heavy when wet. Fine for 1-2 items, not the whole bag
- **Avoid**: Anything that wrinkles badly, needs ironing, or requires dry cleaning

## Climate-Based Packing

### Tropical / Hot & Humid
- Lightweight, breathable fabrics (linen, cotton blends, moisture-wicking)
- Light-colored clothing (reflects heat)
- A rain layer (afternoon showers are common)
- Sunscreen, hat, sunglasses as essential items
- Sandals + one closed-toe shoe for uneven terrain
- **The temple scarf**: A lightweight scarf/sarong covers shoulders and knees for temple visits

### Temperate / European Shoulder Season
- Layering is everything: base layer + mid layer + outer layer
- A packable rain jacket (not an umbrella -- your hands stay free)
- One pair of versatile walking shoes that can also handle a nice restaurant
- Jeans or chinos that work for walking and dinner
- **The 20-degree swing**: Morning might be 10C, afternoon 25C. Layers handle this.

### Cold / Winter
- Thermal base layers (merino wool preferred)
- Insulating mid layer (down or fleece)
- Waterproof/windproof outer shell
- Warm hat, gloves, scarf (these are the difference between miserable and fine)
- Waterproof boots with good traction
- **Pack your heaviest items on the plane**: Wear your coat and boots through the airport

### Desert / Arid
- Sun protection is priority one: hat, sunglasses, long sleeves (yes, long sleeves)
- Light, loose-fitting, breathable clothing
- Layers for dramatic temperature swings (desert nights can be cold)
- Dust protection for electronics
- More water carrying capacity than you think you need

## The Universally Forgotten Items

These make the list because people consistently forget them:

- **Power adapter** (and a USB multi-port charger -- one adapter, charge everything)
- **Headlamp or small flashlight** (navigating dark hostels, power outages, night hikes)
- **Ziplock bags** (wet swimsuits, dirty laundry, leaky bottles, phone protection near water)
- **Earplugs and eye mask** (sleep quality determines trip quality)
- **Copies of documents** (digital AND one printed set, stored separately from originals)
- **A pen** (immigration forms on the plane, journaling, giving your contact to new friends)
- **Day bag / packable backpack** (you need something small for daily exploration)
- **Basic first aid**: pain reliever, anti-diarrheal, band-aids, rehydration salts
- **Snacks for transit**: airports and train stations charge premium. Bring a few bars

## The Laundry Strategy

Laundry is what enables light packing on longer trips:

| Duration | Strategy |
|---|---|
| 1-5 days | No laundry needed if packed right |
| 5-10 days | One sink wash halfway through (quick-dry fabrics) |
| 10-14 days | One laundromat or hotel laundry service |
| 14+ days | Weekly laundry. Most neighborhoods have laundromats. Many hotels and hostels offer laundry |

**Sink wash basics**: Bring a small tube of travel detergent or a few detergent sheets. Wash in the evening, hang to dry, wear the next afternoon.

## Packing Organization

### The Packing Cube System
Organize by type, not by outfit:
- Cube 1: Tops
- Cube 2: Bottoms
- Cube 3: Underwear, socks, sleepwear
- Cube 4: Misc (swimwear, gym clothes, accessories)

Compression cubes reduce volume by 30-50%.

### The "Accessible Items" Principle
These should be reachable without unpacking:
- Passport and travel documents
- Phone charger and adapter
- One change of clothes (in case luggage is delayed)
- Medications
- Snacks and water bottle for transit

## What NOT to Pack

- **"Just in case" items**: If you can't name a specific day you'll use it, leave it
- **Multiple "going out" outfits**: One versatile nice outfit is enough
- **Full-size toiletries**: Buy at the destination or use the accommodation's
- **More than 2 books**: Bring one, trade or buy locally, or use an e-reader
- **Anything irreplaceable**: If you'd be devastated to lose it, leave it home
- **Excessive tech**: Do you really need the laptop, tablet, AND phone? Pick two

## The Final Check

Before closing your bag, ask:
1. Can I carry this comfortably for 15 minutes of walking? (Airport, station, cobblestones)
2. Is there anything I could buy for $10 at the destination instead of packing?
3. Have I left room for things I'll bring home? (Souvenirs, market finds, gifts)
4. If the airline loses my checked bag, do I have one day's essentials in my carry-on?
